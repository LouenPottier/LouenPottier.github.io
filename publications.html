Research Activity

Over the past four years, my research has focused on a central question: how to embed physical priors into neural network architectures for interactive simulation and model-order reduction. Early explorations (Deep Surrogate, GACRNN) established the landscape of the problem. LEBNN emerged as a foundational answer — an interpretable architecture grounded in energy mechanics, later applied in the European ARISE project. This line of work then branched in two directions: LLNN extends LEBNN to nonlinear dynamical systems, while PL-Autoencoder provides the theoretical grounding for structured latent spaces. Both converge into our most ambitious work, Learning Physics from Video.
